Resource Calculation

The DMI resources corresponding to the DCE Credits (MRD,RBR) are sized considering the number of DCE credits. The DCE to DMI connectivity is reduced when the interleaving scheme is identical or partially identical between DCEs and DMIs  : not all the DCEs are connected to all the DMIs. Considering the interleaving scheme will dramatically reduce the DMI resources.

The parameters nDceRbCredits and nDmiMrdCredits must be calculated depending on the commonality in bits in Interleaving.

For Example, if each DCE is allocated 5 credits for the corresponding DMI.

When there is no optimisation , the total buffer size for DCE0 and DCE1 is 

DCE0 → dmi0, dmi1, dmi2, dmi3 → 5+5+5+5 = 20

DCE1 → dmi0, dmi1, dmi2, dmi3 → 5+5+5+5 = 20

However, since we have common select bits, the connection is reduced. and hence the buffer size is also reduced from 20 to 10.

DCE0 → dmi0, dmi1 → 5+5 = 10.

Similarly for DCE1,

DCE1 → dmi2, dmi3 → 5+5 = 10
