Discussion items

TimeItemWhoNotes
Switch only power domainsAll
  • The plan of record is to have adapters at the edge of all power domains. These adapters will be responsible for keeping track of pending transactions, and responding with error responses if a transaction is sent to a domain that is powered down.
20minP channel supportAll
  • Conclusion is to have both P and Q channel on the power interface for each power domains in the NoC.
  • P channel interface will be a simple one, at least for release 1: 2 states: ON, OFF
  • For the Ncore / coherency domain, in the future, there may be more than 2 states. Or, even for the non-coherent fabric, there may be other states defined in the future, pending definition.
35minP/Q active signalsAll
  • Based on the spec, ACTIVE signal has 2 meanings: 1. Wakeup, 2. Busy
  • Busy signal defines whether the power domain is busy (i.e. doing things, or have pending transactions to complete). It is the aggregation of internal states (including the power adapters at the edge of the power domain, interrupt signals, etc.). Which signals are used for the aggregation will be user configurable.
  • Wakeup signalling is confusing - based on the spec, it is the aggregation of whether inputs to this particular domain are active. However, this will cause difficulty in IP implementation, as there will be combinatorial OR of signals coming from different power/clock domains.
    • Conclusion: we will define the Wakeup and Busy signalling as follows:
      • P/Q ACTIVE: a single bit signal, indicating internal busy state
      • P/Q WAKEUP: an Arteris-defined signal. This signal is a vector of N elements. N = number of outputs of this domain to other power domains. This vector will be used as indicator to other domains whether they need to be waken up.
      • By doing this, we are avoiding the combinatorial OR logic inside our IP, but still provide the information that the user will need. It is slightly different from the P/Q channel definition, but our specs and application notes will cover how these signals should be used in the system level PMU
