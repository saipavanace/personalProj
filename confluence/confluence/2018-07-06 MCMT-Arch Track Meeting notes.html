  • Decision - direction to use libnocdata as starting point for Maestro Client Data Model ratified.

Goal: discuss options for Maestro Client DM implementation and ratify one of the direction alternatives.

Architecture work on multiple fronts still needed after this decision.

Discussed DM requirements spec 0.5. Document linked in presentation for everyone to review.

Decision already taken to implement a layer of the DM with OOP paradigm for maximum ease of use for application developers. Will sit on top of libdm (ECS paradigm) with an access layer. Ideally no data duplication at all.

Option 1: extend libdm (ECS) all the way up to the applications. Rejected as too unfamiliar for most developers.

Option 2: write DM from scratch. Could yield best results assuming architecture was stable, but not the case. Also imposes a scheduling constraint on the rest of the development.

Option 3: use libnocdata to implement DM OOP layer.

Federico presents libnocdata pros, cons and risks. Agreement that some of the cons and risks are common to all options ahead.

Extensions and refactorings needed in libnocdata and to be scheduled in roadmap. However, no obvious reasons to assume limitations on lifespan of libnocdata.

libdm-libnocdata access layer to be implemented carefully to meet all constraints.

Initial tentative integration roadmap would allow for complete-stack demo around Sept timeframe.

Attendance ratifies the decision to build on top of libnocdata. Agreement on OOP approach. Further discussions and planning needed to enact integration project.
