30 minConstraints on Burst-size
  • For Legato, 64 bytes is the maximum size of a transaction that can be requested. Since this is specified in bytes, even a 512-bit initiator can have have a narrow-bursts of length > 1.
  • For Presto, not such restriction applies except AXI's 4K boundary restriction, which could be violated by customers by increasing to say 8K. Hence it is best to parameterize this limit.
  • In addition, we need to parameterize the maxBurstLen if it doesn't already exist. maxBurstLen should also be constrained to be never set to less than 16 (AXI restriction).
30 minDecoupling AXI Write Data and Address/Cmd channels
  • The data-packets in the write-data-path in ATUTs need length and offset information to be sent out.This constrains when the requests/commands/can be sent out.
  • The current solution (existing) is to not decouple the two AXI channels, but to have a state-machine in the NL that syncs up both the command/request and data-channels, such that the offset and length information is not lost when the data is being sent out. This solution is good in terms of area, but may not be optimal in terms of performance.
  • The second solution to be thought out is to remove this constraint so that data on the data-channel and commands on the address/request channel can proceed independently. The solution we discussed involves pushing the len and offset info into a FIFO from the request path and popping by the data-path. However this has a bug as Syed and Darshan discussed later in the evening.
  • The problem is - Even with such a FIFO, you'll need to have a single-backpressure in the CTL to both data-path and request-paths on the SMI. But because of back-pressure in the command-path, even though data-path is free in the CTL, the subsequent commands are stopped from entering the CTL. This creates a deadlock scenario.
  • Darshan to brainstorm and find a solution that avoids the problem Syed and he discussed.
