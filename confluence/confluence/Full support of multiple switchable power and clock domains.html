There are 3 successive views of power and clock  configuration

  1. The primary one in Maestro - it eventually determines the other two
  2.  Genwrapper logical structure
  3. UPF file which is derived from genwrapper and other data generated by Maestro


Let's go over a simple example of hw_config_sym_20 to illustrate. It includes 2 switchable power domains, each one encloses one gated clock domain





-Two clocks are highlighted in red and blue.
-Correspondent power domains (derived for instances by tracing clocks) – orange and  lightblue

-3 instances inside dotted line are pma_master->pma_slave->pma_mux , the latter is connected to all atus in the same domain

-In the middle – two networks. Each connected to both clocks and thus associated with both power domains
