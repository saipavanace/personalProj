Symphony allows the user to setup multiple power and clock switching domains and sub-domains. Symphony allows the user to turn these domains on/off using the ARM P and Q channel interfaces. Below are definitions that are useful in understanding how Power and Clock are supported in the Symphony system.

Clocking and Power are defined in terms of regions, domains and sub domains.

  1. A power region represents a group of elements that run off a power supply that is driven by one power source.
  2. A clock region represents a group of elements that a clocked by single clock.
  3. A power domain represents a group of elements that can whose power can be turned on and off.
  4. A clock domains represents a group of elements whose clock can be turned on and off.
  5. There are no power sub domains.
  6. A clock sub domain is a group of elements in a clock domain whose clocks can be turned on and off dynamically as a part of logic function (clock divider).

There are a set of rules that control where regions, domains and sub domains can exist.

  1. Domains must be entirely within a region.
  2. There can multiple domains in a region.
  3. There must be at least one domain in a region.
  4. Sub domains must be entirely within a domain.
  5. There can be multiple subdomains in a domain.
  6. For clocks there must at least one sub domain in a domain.
  7. Clock regions cannot span power regions.
  8. Multiple clock regions can exist in a power region.
  9. There must be at least one clock region in a power region.
  10. Clock domains cannot span power domains.
  11. There can multiple clock domains in a power domain.
  12. There must be at least one clock domain in a power domain.

There are a set of interfaces associated regions and domains.

  1. Every clock sub domain has a clock interface associated with it. A clock interface is composed minimally as a clock signal and a reset signal, but may include more signals, such as a clock enable signal.
  2. Every clock domain that a customer needs to turn on and off has either a P-Channel or Q-Channel interface associated with it.

As you may have noticed, there are no interfaces associated with power regions and domains. To turn a power domain on an off you turn on and off all the clock domains that are enclosed in a power domain, which leads to the rule:

  1. To turn a power domain on and off, all clock domains in a power domain must have a P-Channel or Q-Channel interface.

For Symphony, the architecture does not define nor provide the functionality of a Power Management Unit (PMU). This is considered provided by the customer and the PMU controls the turning on and off of the various domains within Symphony logic through the P-Channel or Q-Channel interfaces provided. For present implementation.

  1. All power control interfaces must be all P-Channel or all Q-Channel.
