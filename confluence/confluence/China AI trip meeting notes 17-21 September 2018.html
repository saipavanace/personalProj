Executive Summary

Visited 9 companies in 5 days in 3 cities to discuss AI, with exception of HiSilicon which was about ISO 26262. China sales and AE team did an excellent job with logistics and providing Benoit, Kurt and Ankit with specific information so that we could prepare properly for each visit. Each meeting was 2 hours or longer.

Key takeaways:

  1. We are correct on our requirementswork regarding the need for regular topologies, HBM2 support, multicast / broadcast stations, source synchronous, and VC-links.
  2. We need to address the issue of Hard Macroswithin regular topologies, either through technology or marketing/positioning. 3 customers talked with us a lot about their perceived need for this. We need to consider this for SYMPHONY, and may consider for a future customer-specific (Iluvatar) FlexNoC 4.x implementation.
  3. Cache coherence/ Ncore generally is not needed in these AI training chips that reside in a data center. This is confirmation of what we already thought.
  4. We need to improve our softwarecapabilities to make it easier for customers to create a good starting point, and faster to create designs with 10s or 100s of initiators and targets. NetSpeed has set a new bar for this with many of these customers, and we will need this to scale adoption of Ncore 3 and SYMPHONY without having to provide tons of support.

Implications for SYMPHONY and Ncore 3:

  1. Need to build rings, meshes and torithat are efficient – this requires new HW (in addition to SW): new “router” switch, scalable routing function for very large meshes (address vs. ID based routing)
  2. Software needs to provide good starting pointfor any design. Needs to match customer's requirements more than FlexNoC's current "start with a crossbar" approach.
  3. Software needs to scale to handle very large designsin a very user-responsive, fast manner. FlexNoC chokes above an 8x8 mesh, or so. Most designs larger than this will have to use Ncore 3 and SYMPHONY.
  4. Need to addressHard Macrorequirements.

Attendees:

Benoit went over Arteris product presentation – targeted towards AI market.

 

Good discussions during the presentation especially with William/Rick/Gabriel. Here are takeaways from the meeting:

  1. Their current product doesn’t have interconnect from us or NetSpeed. 
    1. They have licensed NetSpeed for next two projects.

    1. We reiterated potential dismal NetSpeed support going forward, and pointed out NetSpeed's weakness for  ISO 26262. They seems to understand. Kurt forwarded his ISO 26262 - people, product, process paper to Frank.

Cambricon

Wednesday, 19 September 2018, Beijing

Attendees: 

  • Cambricon - Paddy (Sourcing Engineer), and 3 others (no cards)
  • Arteris - Rick, Gabriel, Benoit, Kurt, Ankit

 

First Project - uses Arteris FlexNoC

  • Most things going well
  • Only issue is timing closure in physical design with their large floorplan

 

Second Project - using NetSpeed

  • Changed for technical reasons - more complex chip; 10s of masters, 10s of slaves
    • Approach - NetSpeed is switch-based NoC rather than link-based like FlexNoC. Perceived advantage with larger, regular topology designs.
    • Wider data paths - 512 bit, but has 1024 bit wish (FlexNoC already has these)
    • NIU size - perceives that NIUs become too large with increase in number of outstanding transactions and subsequent increase in NIU OTT
      • Discussion on how this is normal for any technology. They didn't provide any insight into how NetSpeed does this or how NetSpeed is better at this.
      • GUI speed - FlexNoC ran slowly with large design (every change results in complete design recheck before UI updates). 

 

Learning and Feedback from FlexNoC Experience

  • Physical design - partitioned NoC in to 2 to go through synthesis
    • Request and response networks had different sizes (expected)
    • Closed timing on two partitions independently
  • Error Logging - response header read
    • Is already option in FlexNoC - wasn't aware that he can remove it 
    • We asked information on cost of error logging logic area vs. NetSpeed but no answer

 

Discussion of SequenceID - is 8 bits too small?

  • Initiator NIU ID mapping
  • Put additional bits into user bits rather than putting extraneous info into sequenceID

 

CodaCache - lots of discussion!

  1. Thinks 512 Byte cache line size is important
  2. Asked if we support:
    1. Partial AXI writes? Yes
    2. Read-modify-write? Yes. Also discussed how many R-M-W we can do.
  3. Asked about obtaining cache misses outstanding during runtime

 

Source-Synchronous Component - strong interest

  1. Asked when available - October
  2. Flow Control - discussion on how it is done. 2 options:
    1. Credits - doesn't require large buffers, but must skew manage the wires
    2. On/Off - single wire, but deeper buffers
  3. Comparison to Sonics meso-synchronous logic - they asked about this
    1. Benoit explained that we are different:
      1.                                                i.           Meso-synchronous - 1 PLL with clocks in any phase
      2.                                               ii.           Source-synchronous - unique to Arteris; required backend knowledge to implement

 

VC-Links (in addition to rdy/vld)

  1. Deadlocks - discussion on deadlocks with Ring topologies. We explained that we are non-blocking and guarantee progress.
  2. Flow Control - they asked if it is credit-based and Benoît explained that it is,
    1. Discussed future option of combining source-synchronous components with VC-Links

 

Other new FlexNoC 4 AI features discussed

  1. Multicast / Broadcast stations - not much interest / discussion
  2. Cache Coherence - not using. NUMA is inefficient for their architecture
  3. Rings - said GUI top view needs improvement
  4. Meshes -finds interesting
  5. Routing - would like based on node location rather than address-only

 

NetSpeed comparison - what do they think is better than Arteris?

  1. Simpler to get started with NetSpeed
    1. Easy to input "key points" of a design into tool and get a response
    2. But, Arteris configuration capabilities are more rich
  2. NetSpeed targeted server / high end market at the very start, so better match of IP to Cambricon use cases - switches
  3. Physical timing closure was hard with FlexNoC
    1. But, NetSpeed chip is not yet taped out so don't know if timing is easier
  4. Automation - Although they like the better starting point of NetSpeed, they don't trust the automation in tools 100%. They prefer manual configuration to complete automation.

 

Cambricon wants:

  1. Faster tool
  2. Easier control and visibility in tooling
  3. Smaller area - reiterated OTT size in NIU for large number of outstanding transactions, had to turn on all pipelines for timing closure
    1. Perceives that NetSpeed is better at this, even though we think this can not be true
  4. Likes our AI features!

Tencent

Friday, 21 September 2018, Shanghai

Attendees: 

  • Tencent - 2 people
  • Arteris - Gary, Gabriel, Benoit, Kurt, Ankit

 

TEG business group - support cloud, gaming, biz groups. 2,000 people. Provides architectures for storage, video codec, networking, homogeneous computing

 

Goal: Transform existing AI algos into an ASIC

  • Homogeneous AI core for first ASIC
  • Design has already been verified in FPGA and deployed in data center

 

Protocol discussion

  1. AXI Stream - we don't support because we are address based, not ID-based
    1. Tencent person thinks this is important for AI
  2. Benefits of Arteris if using same protocol?
    1. Even if same protocol, if domain crossing or large designs, we are better than NIC crossbars.
    2. Arm Asynch boundaries (ADB-400) are very expensive area-wise. Arm at transaction boundary, Arteris is at transport level. More efficient!
    3. Arteris is usually 30-40% smaller if have power domain crossings

 

Ncore / cache coherence - sees no need today. SW manages data movement.

 

CodaCache - some interest

  • Discussion of cache line size, sized based on burst length from memory
  • Way partitioning - allocate cache ways to groups of masters to avoid thrashing

 

HBM2 support and interleaving

  • Like interleaving approach because they have multiple groups that are not synched and don't know what the traffic will be.
  • Back pressure - We have it. VLD/RDY back pressure description and explanation of reorder buffers (RoB) for responses,
  • Can use with multiple HMB2 dice suing (1) interleaving, or (2) channel selection bits

 

Broadcast station

  • Likes concept for sharing weights and other data

 

Other items:

  • Asked about GDDR vs. HBM2, but is not using GDDR, yet.
  • Will use design services company for backend but hasn't picked one, yet.
