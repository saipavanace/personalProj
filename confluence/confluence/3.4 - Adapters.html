There are Unidirectional Adapters and Bidirectional Adapters as shown below:

Unidirectional adapters are positioned between ATP links and do some form of adaption. They might convert width, packet style, clock or add pipe stages.

Bidirectional adapters are positioned between two sets of ATP links, request and response. The responses for all the requests that travel over the request links must travel back over the response links. An example of a bidirectional adapter is an adapter that is positioned between clock domains.
