Interface Parity: All input signals have duplicated “_chk” signals associated with them, which are used to drive “odd parity“ bits. When wrong values are driven on the “_chk“ signals, we log an error. However, to simulate a parity mismatch, we just toggle the bits in the “_chk“ signals and, to avoid complications, we don’t introduce errors in the actual signals itself

I spoke to the designer, Darshan Alagud , and we both agree that it is a low risk when we don’t introduce error on the actual signals
