30minNvidia meeting pass downJoe Butler (Deactivated)
  • Goal of the meeting was to get them interested and want to learn more (and of course, ultimately become an early access customer!)
  • Would also like them to sign the next level of NDA
  • Chief architects from NV attended the meeting (Franck Seigneret and Mike Ditty)
  • Clarified to NV that release 1 of symphony is going to target city driving ADAS, rather than just Ncore transport interconnect
  • NV expressed interest in:
    • Customization - protocol adaptation as well as inserting custom IP in fabric
    • Functional safety; error management/fail operational
    • Physical implementation: their backend team has way to optimize macros; with FlexNoC, each component is optimized for area and hence there are rarely any units are the same. Therefore, backend cannot apply their techniques
    • SCRIPTING!!! (for both front-end input, e.g. memory map, and backend, e.g. adding pipeline stages)
    • Automation - FlexNoC currently requires users to go too "low level" to design the interconnect. Very interested in higher level of automation.
    • NoC-to-NoC connections and the corresponding automation (they have many NoCs)
    • Clock forwarding (source synchronous) is used to cross distances in their chip (not supported in FlexNoC)
    • Virtual channel
    • VIRTUALIZATION!!! (they have 8 virtual machines running and would like them to not interfere with each other)
    • ARM tooling support (meta data; compatible with Socrates)
    • Firewalls - should be backward compatible with FlexNoC (custom RTL)
  • Not much interest in:
    • CCIX and die-to-die communication
    • OCP protocol support (they are all AMBA based)
